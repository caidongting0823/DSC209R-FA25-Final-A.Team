Project Description

This final project extends our previous Project 3 (US 100yrs Earthquakes Visualization) by shifting focus to a narrative-driven exploration of major historical earthquake(s) (# depending on time) in the United States. The current plan is to showcase either the 1989 Loma Prieta earthquake or the 1994 Northridge earthquake, presenting it as a chronological account of seismic events.

Users will be able to watch the chosen earthquake unfold along a timeline with synchronized maps, charts, and embedded historical media. The interface will support an autoplay (like the “roll play mode” from Project 3) that plays through the event from foreshocks to main shock to aftershocks, as well as manual navigation for users who wish to explore at their own pace. The experience will highlight the temporal progression and spatial patterns of the seismic activity – for example, illustrating how shock waves propagate and how aftershocks cluster over time. Our goal is to deepen user engagement and understanding of how a major earthquake unfolds, connecting raw seismic data to a compelling historical narrative.

This final project is inspired by the MBTA subway visualization and Explorable Explanations.

Proposed Visualizations & Story Elements in Detail

  • Timeline-Based Story Mode: A play-by-play timeline of the earthquake event, from any foreshocks through the main shock and subsequent aftershocks. This timeline can autoplay the sequence of events or allow users to scrub through time manually, providing a clear chronological narrative of the earthquake’s progression.
  • Integrated Media and Annotations: Contextual media (such as historical photographs, news footage, or diagrams) will be embedded alongside the visualization. As the timeline progresses, relevant images, video clips, and text annotations will appear, giving users historical perspective and explanations (e.g. damage scenes, notable reactions) that enrich the data with storytelling.
  • Seismic Waveform & Impact Visualization: Additional graphics will illustrate patterns in the earthquake’s seismic data and impacts. For example, a simple waveform or shaking-intensity chart could run in parallel with the timeline, showing the intensity of the tremors over time. This element will help highlight how the main shock’s energy was released and how it was recorded or felt at various locations.
  • User Controls and Navigation: The interface will include user-friendly controls to toggle between an automated “play” mode and a manual exploration mode. Users can pause, rewind, or jump to specific story milestones (e.g. the moment of the main shock or a significant aftershock). We also plan to include toggle options to switch between viewing aggregate patterns (clustering of events) and individual event details, giving both a macro and micro perspective of the earthquake sequence.
  • Project 3 Integration Improvements of “Story Mode”: Display major earthquake groups on a map, provide a direct review (since there has been no response yet), and allow users to click on the group to learn more about the relevant earthquake event. After clicking on a group of earthquakes, the map will zoom in to show only the relevant earthquake event. Each earthquake event within the group will still be interactive and display information. Additionally, a sub-page will pop up on the slide showing the event details. This will be integrated and linked to our final project page.
